Order was built to simplify buying things for businesses. Nine years in, we've learned that the complexity doesn't live in one screen. It lives across hundreds of vendor portals, thousands of approval rules, and millions of transactions that each have their own edge cases.

We built Agentic Labs to attack that complexity with a different kind of tool.

## What Agentic Labs is

Agentic Labs is Order's AI research and deployment division. We build autonomous agents that execute real procurement work: logging into vendor portals, comparing prices, placing orders, routing approvals, detecting anomalies, and handling exceptions.

These aren't chatbots sitting in front of a dashboard. They're systems that do the work.

## Why internal first

Before deploying agents into customer environments, we run them inside Order. Our own procurement operations, our own approval workflows, our own vendor management. This isn't a testing phase. It's the proving ground.

If we can't run these systems internally with reliability, permissioning, auditability, and measurable ROI, the external product stays theoretical.

## The operating thesis

Buying complexity is distributed. It spans vendor coordination, policy enforcement, intake, purchasing, payment, exception handling, and follow-up. Traditional software made those steps visible. We're building infrastructure that executes them.

The first wedge is procurement. The surface area is large, the data is structured, and Order already has permission to sit in the workflow. Agentic Labs turns that position into an execution layer.

## What's next

We'll be publishing technical research on this blog: how we train agents on real transaction data, how we handle vendor portal automation across 500+ unique interfaces, and how we build reliable systems that fail gracefully when they encounter the unexpected.

If you're working on similar problems or want to learn more, reach out.
